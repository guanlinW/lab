Institute of Metabolism & Integrative Biology

Wang Lab @ Fudan University

We are a research group at the Institute of Metabolism and Integrative Biology (IMIB), Fudan University. Our lab integrates computational biology, artificial intelligence, single-cell omics, and spatial transcriptomics to decode metabolic programs, cellular states, and disease mechanisms across obesity, fibrosis, hematopoietic aging, and cancer.

Research

What We Do

We combine state-of-the-art single-cell omics, spatial transcriptomics, long-read sequencing, and computational modeling to investigate metabolism and disease biology at cellular resolution.

Decoding Metabolic Signatures Across Complex Diseases

Applying multi-omics approaches to dissect the molecular underpinnings of obesity, metabolic syndrome, and cancer. We leverage large-scale datasets to identify metabolic reprogramming signatures, uncover how metabolic dysregulation drives disease progression, and reveal potential therapeutic vulnerabilities.

Pan-fibrosis Therapeutic Discovery

Using single-cell and spatial technologies to map shared and disease-specific cellular mechanisms across fibrotic disorders, identify conserved therapeutic targets, and accelerate pan-fibrosis biomarker and treatment discovery.

Computational Tools & Digital Metabolism

Building novel algorithms, digital metabolism frameworks, and integrative multi-modal analysis pipelines to identify metabolic fingerprints, cellular states, and biologically interpretable disease programs from large-scale omics data.

SURF

A self-supervised deep learning method for reference-free deconvolution in spatial transcriptomics. SURF integrates high-dimensional gene data analysis with self-supervised deep learning to effectively model nonlinear gene interactions and leverage spot relationships.

SourcePaper
>_

snRNA-seq Workflow

An optimized upstream analytical workflow for single-nucleus transcriptomics in main metabolic tissues (adipose tissue, muscle, liver, and hypothalamus). A standard protocol for researchers seeking to apply snRNA-seq to metabolic tissues.
