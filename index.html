Institute of Metabolism & Integrative Biology

Welcome to Wang Lab @ Fudan University

We are a research group at the Institute of Metabolism and Integrative Biology (IMIB), Fudan University. Our lab combines computational biology, single-cell omics, spatial transcriptomics, and artificial intelligence to decode metabolism signatures and molecular programs across complex human diseases. We aim to uncover the mechanistic basis of obesity, fibrosis, hematopoietic aging, and cancer through integrative multi-omics and scalable data-driven models.

Highlights

Research Overview

Our work lies at the interface of metabolism, omics technologies, and computational biology.

Direction 01

Digital Metabolism

We develop computational frameworks to quantify and model metabolic states from large-scale single-cell, spatial, and multi-omics datasets across tissues and diseases.

Direction 02

Single-cell & Spatial Atlases

We build high-resolution reference atlases of adipose tissue, hematopoiesis, and disease ecosystems to identify cell states, trajectories, and microenvironmental interactions.

Direction 03

AI for Complex Disease Biology

We integrate machine learning, deep learning, and graph-based modeling with biological knowledge to discover actionable mechanisms in obesity, fibrosis, and cancer.

Research

What We Do

We focus on the following directions, combining state-of-the-art single-cell omics, long-read sequencing, spatial transcriptomics, and computational methods.

Theme 1

Decoding Metabolic Signatures Across Complex Diseases

We apply multi-omics approaches to dissect the molecular underpinnings of obesity, metabolic disorders, fibrosis, and cancer. By integrating transcriptomic, spatial, and population-scale data, we identify metabolic reprogramming signatures and reveal potential therapeutic vulnerabilities.

Theme 2

Fibrosis and Tissue Remodeling

Using single-cell and spatial technologies, we map the cellular ecosystems of fibrotic diseases, identify key driver populations, and characterize the signaling circuits that govern tissue remodeling and chronic pathology.

Theme 3

Computational Tools & Data Integration

We build novel algorithms and integrative frameworks for large-scale multi-modal data analysis, enabling robust discovery of disease-associated states, metabolism fingerprints, and biologically interpretable biomarkers.

SURF

A self-supervised deep learning method for reference-free deconvolution in spatial transcriptomics. SURF integrates high-dimensional gene-expression modeling with self-supervised representation learning to resolve spot-level cell composition and states.
