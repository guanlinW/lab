Institute of Metabolism & Integrative Biology

Welcome to Wang Lab @ Fudan University

We are a research group at the Institute of Metabolism and Integrative Biology (IMIB), Fudan University. Our lab combines computational biology and state-of-the-art omics technologies (single-cell omics and spatial transcriptomics) to decode metabolism signatures and fingerprints from large-scale datasets. We aim to understand the molecular basis of complex traits and diseases including obesity, fibrosis, and cancer through cutting-edge genomic and computational approaches.

Research

What We Do

We focus on the following directions, combining state-of-the-art single-cell omics, long-read sequencing, spatial transcriptomics, and computational methods.

Decoding Metabolic Signatures Across Complex Diseases

Applying multi-omics approaches to dissect the molecular underpinnings of obesity, metabolic syndrome, and cancer. We leverage large-scale datasets to identify metabolic reprogramming signatures, uncover how metabolic dysregulation drives disease progression, and reveal potential therapeutic vulnerabilities.

Fibrosis Mechanisms

Using single-cell and spatial technologies to map the cellular landscapes of fibrotic diseases, identifying key driver populations and therapeutic targets in tissue remodeling.

Computational Tools & Data Integration

Building novel algorithms and integrative frameworks to analyze and integrate multi-modal datasets across tissues, species, and conditions, enabling robust discoveries of metabolic fingerprints and biomarker signatures from large-scale single-cell and spatial datasets.

Tools & Software

SURF

A self-supervised deep learning method for reference-free deconvolution in spatial transcriptomics. SURF integrates high-dimensional gene data analysis with self-supervised deep learning to effectively model nonlinear gene interactions and leverage spot relationships.

SourcePaper
>_

snRNA-seq Workflow

An optimized upstream analytical workflow for single-nucleus transcriptomics in main metabolic tissues (adipose tissue, muscle, liver, and hypothalamus). A standard protocol for researchers seeking to apply snRNA-seq to metabolic tissues.
